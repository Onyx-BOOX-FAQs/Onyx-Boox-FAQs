General Questions

E-paper (often called E-Ink) uses tiny microcapsules with black and white particles that physically move to form text and images. Once the page is displayed, it doesn’t need power to stay visible. While LCD and OLED screens emit light directly toward your eyes. Pixels refresh many times per second to create smooth motion and color.

Onyx is the company behind BOOX devices. They specialize in E-Ink tablets and eReaders designed for reading, note-taking, and productivity.

Other ePaper products are primarily focused only on reading, sometimes locked behind a proprietary ecosystem, while BOOX devices offer more flexibility with Android apps, note-taking, file support, and productivity features.

BOOX devices use E-Ink screens, which are easier on the eyes and provide better battery life. They are designed for reading, writing, and focused productivity rather than gaming or video-heavy use.
